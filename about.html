About Me

I have been a runner, a blogger, a drawer and a clarinet player; I wrote Chinese calligraphy; I played piano and clarinet; I assembled radio receivers. However, finally I chose to be a UX designer. It’s fun.

With over 6 years of experience on advertising and marketing, I analyze customer needs and provide strategic solutions. But now, I want to put my emphasis on people. I like people, and observing people. I care about their requirements and notice their pain points. What makes UX attractive is that every change you make would affect people’s behavior, change their way of thinking.

No matter how small the change is, it would make my day.
